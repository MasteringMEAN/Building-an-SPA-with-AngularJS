About the Author

Raoni Boaventura is a frontend engineer who has been working with JavaScript and JavaScript frameworks from the very beginning of his career, about 8 years ago. He has a strong academic background and a degree in Computer Science. He has worked for several companies and clients around the world, completing a large number of front¬end and Full Stack projects.

He started working with AngularJS about two years ago and immediately fell in love with it. Since that time, he has not only been involved in multiple AngularJS projects with multinational clients but has also been an active peer in the AngularJS community, having published key articles and tutorials about the framework.



About the Reviewers

Jose Dunia is an experienced web developer with a passion for computer graphics and user interfaces. He would like to see software, especially video games and simulations, being used more within the various levels of education. He started developing web projects at the age of 12, learning through JavaScript, the basic principles of programming. His interest for software lead him to pursue a B.E. in Computer Engineering at the Universidad Simón Bolívar. He holds an M.S. in Digital Arts and Sciences from the University of Florida, where he studied computer graphics and serious games.

Currently, he is working for Shadow Health, a start-up that designs and develops interactive simulations for the advancement of health education. He is the Lead Web Developer for Shadow Health's incubator projects. He has been using AngularJS for more than a year. He has the ability to do quick iterations and build interfaces in a short span of time while still meeting business requirements that ask for user friendly experiences, which makes AngularJS his preferred JavaScript framework.


Vincenzo Ferrari, better known as Wilk, is a Full Stack web developer and an enthusiastic open source supporter. He has been working with several technologies, both on the backend and frontend to face many situations and problems, with different programming languages, such as PHP, Java, Python, and JavaScript. He's has a strong hold over the JavaScript language, and he has been developing many open source libraries for the Sencha, ExtJS, and AngularJS frameworks. He currently conducts lots of conferences and workshops in Italy on web technologies; he works as a software engineer at Ibuildings Italia.


Evgeny Goldin is a Ruby, Groovy, and Scala software developer who turned into an automation and release engineer to introduce order where chaos usually reigns. On an average day, all things related to cloud, automation, and continuous delivery get his immediate attention. Back home, he's a proud father to a 1-year-old son, dreaming of a day when a proper tech talk will happen between the two. When he has any spare time, he explores the subjects of aviation safety, functional programming, and web security. He has worked for IBM, BMC, Thomson Reuters, and JetBrains. He has also worked on the books Mastering Chef, Mastering AWS Development, Learning Continuous Integration with TeamCity, all by Packt Publishing.
